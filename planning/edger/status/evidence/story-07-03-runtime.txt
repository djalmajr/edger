## Behavior covered

- Enabled `manifest.cron[]` jobs are collected and scheduled.
- `@every` short intervals dispatch within the integration-test tolerance.
- Simple minute cron schedules such as `*/1 * * * *` are accepted by the v1
  parser.
- Internal cron requests include `x-edger-internal: true`,
  pipeline-bound `Authorization: Bearer $ROOT_API_KEY`, and a cron request id.
- Worker code receives the internal marker but not the root `Authorization`
  header.
- Spoofed `x-edger-internal` headers on public worker requests are stripped
  before user code.
- The worker receives the rewritten job-local path, for example `/tick`.
- Disabled workers do not register cron jobs; re-enable requires reload.
- Invalid schedules fail validation with `CRON_SCHEDULE_INVALID`.
- Missing `ROOT_API_KEY` with jobs fails startup with `CRON_AUTH_MISSING`.
- Shutdown stops scheduler tasks and no further ticks are observed after join.
- `/metrics` exposes `edger_cron_executions_total`.
